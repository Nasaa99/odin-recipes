Pizza

Description

Homemade pepperoni pizza is a delicious, family-friendly dish that turns any ordinary night into a pizza party. This version features a golden, crispy crust, a generous layer of mozzarella, and a clever “Bonus Pepperoni” trick—half the pepperoni is chopped and layered under the cheese for extra flavor in every bite. Whether using store-bought or homemade dough, this recipe adapts easily to your kitchen setup, with baking options using a pizza stone or cast iron skillet.

The pizza is cooked at a high temperature to achieve a perfectly crisp crust, and it's topped with your favorite thin-sliced pepperoni for that signature charred finish. As a fun twist, it's served with optional honey butter for dipping the crust—a kid-approved touch inspired by Colorado-style pizza. With just 25 minutes of total cook time, it'ss a quick, satisfying alternative to delivery that delivers on both taste and texture.

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 18 to 20 slices pepperoni
  • 12 ounces mozzarella cheese, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon fresh oregano (optional)
  • Flour (for rolling and shaping dough)

For the Quick Pizza Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (no salt)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated onion
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil

For the Honey Butter (Optional):

  • 1/4 cup softened butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 500°F: If using a pizza stone, preheat it in the oven for at least 20 minutes so it gets nice and hot.
  2. Make the sauce: Stir together all sauce ingredients if you're using the homemade version. The recipe makes enough for one large pizza.
  3. Roll out the dough: Roll the dough on a lightly floured surface to a 14-inch circle. If it's hard to roll, let it rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the toppings: Transfer the dough to a pizza peel or cast iron skillet. Spread the sauce, add chopped pepperoni under the cheese, then top with more cheese and remaining pepperoni. Season with black pepper.
  5. Cook the pizza: If using a stone, cook the pizza in the center of the stone for 6 minutes, rotate it, then bake another 6–8 minutes. For a skillet, heat it on the stove for 2 minutes, then bake in the oven for 10–12 minutes until the crust is golden brown.
  6. Slice and serve: Let the pizza rest briefly, slice it, and optionally top with fresh oregano. Serve warm with a side salad if desired.
  7. Make the honey butter (optional): Stir together softened butter and honey. Serve as a dip for the crust.
  8. Store leftovers: Keep leftovers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in a skillet with oil over medium heat and cover until the cheese melts and the crust crisps.
